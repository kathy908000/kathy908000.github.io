Projects

Population Segmentation For Health System Management

The objective is to identify high resource use patients and to predict the length of stay and total cost of patients. Furthermore, we want to explore whether clustering the patients into “homogeneous” groups can yield better predictions than in the general body. We will use models including regression models, random forest, k-means clustering, and hierarchical clustering. We will also use models developed for population segmentation including sparse tree ensembles. This research project is part of the Canada Ontario healthcare population segmentation project launched by Dr. Laura Rosella in 2020. The result of the research will also be examined and used for Canada to optimize health resource allocation.

Fake News Detection

The Fake News Challenge was a 2017 competition to detect stances of news articles.The overall task was to classify a news article headline and body pair into one of four categories: unrelated, agree, disagree, or discuss. We split this into two separate tasks for relevance and stance detection, and trained separate models for each task. We decided to develop our own approach for this task, with the goal of creating visualizations to make our models and results more transparent and interpretable.
